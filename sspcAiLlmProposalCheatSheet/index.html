The Core Challenge: The Cost-Privacy Dilemma

Enterprises face a painful choice: invest in expensive, high-maintenance GPU infrastructure to run LLMs in-house, or rely on risky third-party APIs that send proprietary data to external servers [1][2]. Our research presents a "Third Path" ‚Äî a CPU-only, RAG-augmented architecture that is 5.8√ó cheaper than a V100 GPU equivalent, achieves a 21-second mean response time, and keeps 100% of data within the organisation's secure cloud tenant.

Proven Research Pillars ‚Äî From Our Published Work

CPU-Only Inference

The "Third Path" ‚Äî Proven

We demonstrated that a 4-bit quantized Gemma 2B model running on standard CPUs via llama-cpp-python achieves a 21-second mean response time for FAQ workloads. Our architecture is 5.8√ó more cost-effective than a comparable V100 GPU setup ‚Äî validated in a live production deployment.

llama.cpp 4-bit GGUF 5.8√ó Savings

RAG for Accuracy

The Key Enabler

Our RAG pipeline using FAISS vector search enables a smaller, faster model to perform accurately by grounding every response in narrow, relevant context retrieved from an internal knowledge base [3][4][5]. This is the architectural breakthrough that makes CPU-only inference viable for enterprise use.

FAISS Vector Search Context Grounding

Validated in Production

Not Theoretical ‚Äî Deployed

This system is not a simulation. It is a live, deployed FAQ system benchmarked with a mean response time of 21 seconds and a monthly operational cost of ~$204 on Azure App Service. The findings are documented in a peer-reviewed submission, proving real-world viability.

Azure App Service ~$204/mo Live System

Why Now? The Convergence of Opportunity

Enterprise LLM Adoption Barriers

Industry reports consistently cite cost and data privacy as the #1 and #2 obstacles to enterprise LLM adoption [1]. Our architecture directly solves both.

Quantization & RAG Maturity

Breakthroughs in 4-bit quantization (GGUF format) [6][7] and mature RAG tooling (FAISS, llama.cpp) have made CPU-only inference a practical reality for the first time.

EU AI Act & GDPR Pressure

The EU AI Act mandates data governance for high-risk AI systems. Our "data-stays-local" architecture is compliant by design, not by retrofit.
